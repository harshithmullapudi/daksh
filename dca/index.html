Daksh Campus Ambassador Initiative

As one of the largest techno-management fest in South India, Daksh is one of the main attractions of SASTRA University. As we expand, we want to include more students in our extravaganza. To endorse Daksh, we are now teaming up with students from various colleges under our Campus Ambassador Program.

Why should you be the campus ambassador for Daksh?

This project is quite arduous, but one must know with great efforts comes great rewards. Campus ambassadors gain tremendous confidence in soft skills and social media handling. Being a part of Daksh will help you improve various leadership and marketing skills, igniting the potential in you.

With a keen interest to grow and enjoy, Daksh Campus Ambassador Program is here to quench your thirst.

Daksh Campus Ambassadors can contact any other campus ambassador or a representative from SASTRA, thereby maintaining a healthy relationship across various campuses in the country.

Responsibilities

You will be the representative of Daksh in and around your campus. This will put you in touch with other Ambassadors, thus expanding your perspective and experience that you acquire.Â  You will have to develop a campus community to notify our activities in your college, promote the brand on campus through events and social media, allure participants for the fest and host various events and workshops at your college in collaboration with our team.

Who can apply?

If you
  1. Are available for the duration of 4 months, from November to February,
  2. Are active on social media, and
  3. Are currently studying or have graduated.

Benefits

  1. Official certification from Daksh.
  2. Goodies and Daksh tees.
  3. Discount on accommodation during the fest.
  4. Free passes to pro-shows during Daksh.

Additional incentives

  1. Achievable internship on interns-fair during Daksh
  2. complimentary passes to power-talks during Daksh

How does it work?

CAs are assigned diverse tasks from time to time. These tasks range from publicizing to organizing workshops. Every task carries a fixed number of points, which are awarded upon completion of the task. For example, one post shared on Facebook will give the CA one point. Tasks which require more efforts like publicizing on-campus will carry more points.
